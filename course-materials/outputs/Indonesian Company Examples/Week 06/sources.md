### Accounting Standards Referenced
- **PSAK 72** (IFRS 15) — Revenue from Contracts with Customers: five-step model, multiple performance obligations, variable consideration, principal vs agent
- **PSAK 5** (IFRS 8) — Operating Segments: segment reporting requirements
- **PSAK 16** (IAS 16) — Property, Plant and Equipment: depreciation methods, useful life estimates
- **PSAK 19** (IAS 38) — Intangible Assets: amortization of spectrum licenses and software
- **PSAK 46** (IAS 12) — Income Taxes: deferred tax assets and liabilities
- **PSAK 24** (IAS 19) — Employee Benefits: pension and post-employment benefits
- **PSAK 48** (IAS 36) — Impairment of Assets: impairment testing for network assets

### Notes
- All financial data sourced from publicly available audited consolidated financial statements filed with Indonesia Stock Exchange (IDX) and/or US SEC.
- Revenue segment breakdowns are based on Telkom's official segment reporting categories as disclosed in the Annual Report 2023 and notes to financial statements.
- Revenue segment figures are approximate and may reflect rounding; refer to primary sources for exact figures.
- Operating profit figures for 2021 and 2022 are from comparative data in the 2023 audited financial statements.
- EBITDA is a non-GAAP measure reported by Telkom in its Annual Report management discussion section.
- D&A figure of Rp 27.4 trillion for 2023 includes depreciation of property, plant & equipment and amortization of intangible assets (primarily spectrum licenses and software).
- The IndiHome integration into Telkomsel was completed in 2023; some financial impacts of the restructuring may be classified as non-recurring but were not separately quantified in publicly available summaries.
- Sub-component breakdowns within COGS (network costs, interconnection costs, content costs) are estimated based on disclosures in the notes to financial statements; not all sub-items are disclosed at the same granularity.
- Ratios are approximate, computed from rounded figures in published financial statements.
- Hypotheses regarding 2022 margin decline are analytical interpretations based on publicly available information; they have not been confirmed by management.
