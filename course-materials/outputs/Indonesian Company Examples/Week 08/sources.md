### Notes
- All financial data sourced from publicly available audited consolidated financial statements filed with Indonesia Stock Exchange (IDX).
- Cash flow figures are from the Consolidated Statement of Cash Flows in ICBP's audited financial statements for FY2021, FY2022, and FY2023.
- ICBP uses PSAK (Indonesian adoption of IFRS) and classifies interest paid under financing activities (CFF), not operating activities. This is a permitted choice under PSAK 2 (IAS 7). Analysts comparing with US GAAP companies should adjust accordingly.
- The Pinehill acquisition value of ~USD 2.99 billion is based on ICBP's official disclosure to IDX in 2020. The exact Rupiah equivalent depends on the exchange rate at the time of the transaction.
- CFO reconciliation items for 2023 are approximate figures based on the structure of the Statement of Cash Flows in the audited financial statements. Exact line items may differ slightly due to rounding and aggregation in published statements.
- Segment revenue and operating margin estimates are based on segment reporting disclosures in the Annual Report 2023. Operating margins are estimated from segment profit/loss data and may not precisely match company-reported figures due to allocation methodologies.
- FCFF is computed as CFO − Capex. Since ICBP classifies interest payments in CFF (not CFO), this FCFF calculation already reflects cash flows before interest payments to debtholders, making it appropriate for enterprise valuation using WACC.
- FCFE is computed as FCFF − Net Debt Repayment − Interest Paid, reflecting cash available to equity holders after all obligations.
- Cash Flow Adequacy ratio is computed as CFO / (Capex + Net Debt Repayment + Dividends). The denominator represents the three primary cash requirements: reinvestment, debt service, and shareholder returns.
- All ratios are approximate, computed from rounded figures in published financial statements.
- Debt figures refer to total interest-bearing debt (bank loans + bonds), not total liabilities.
